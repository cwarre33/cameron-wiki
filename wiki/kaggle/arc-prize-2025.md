# ARC Prize 2025

**Competition:** ARC Prize 2025
**Prize:** $1,000,000 | **Deadline:** 2025-11-03 (closed)

## What it is

The public Kaggle competition associated with the ARC Prize — $1M to achieve 85%+ on ARC-AGI-2. The benchmark tests abstract reasoning on novel visual grid puzzles that cannot be solved by memorization. See [[wiki/benchmarks/arc-agi.md]].

## Cameron's connection

Cameron built [[wiki/kaggle/arc-agi-benchmarking.md]] — a multi-provider async test harness for running frontier LLMs against ARC-AGI tasks. His notebook `submission-loopassembly-score-34-7` suggests he was running automated submission loops.

The "Loopassembly" naming suggests an assembly of inference loops — possibly combining multiple LLM attempts, majority voting, or iterative refinement per task.

## Context

ARC Prize 2025 was the second year of the competition. The $1M grand prize required 85% on ARC-AGI-2 — a bar no team reached in 2025. The prize went unclaimed. Top public submissions scored in the 40–50% range.

Cameron's approach (LLM-based inference via his benchmarking harness) reflects the dominant strategy of the competition: frontier LLMs with various prompting and ensemble strategies.
